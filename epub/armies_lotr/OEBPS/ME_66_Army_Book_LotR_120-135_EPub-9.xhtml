Lothlórien

To the east of the Misty Mountains and on the western bank of the Anduin lies the wooded Elven realm of Lothlórien, home of the Galadhrim Elves. Unlike the dark, twisted forest of Mirkwood, Lothlórien is a place of light, sanctuary and healing; one where only the closest allies of the Elves are allowed to tread. Its paths are kept clear of the servants of the enemy, making Lothlórien one of the few safe havens in Middle-earth during the Third Age.

Lothlórien itself is ruled over by Galadriel and Celeborn, two Elves of immense power that have walked Middle-earth since before the years of the First Age. Galadriel is a mighty sorceress whose power can rival any in Middle-earth, and she is able to keep the borders of her lands safe through the power of Nenya, one of the three Elven Rings. Few would dare to stand against the might of the Lady Galadriel, and those who would will find themselves on the receiving end of her wrath.

Yet Lothlórien cannot be kept safe by the power of Galadriel alone. It is through the martial prowess of the Galadhrim warriors that the woodland realm has been kept free from marauding Orc bands; something that has become increasingly more common as the years of the Third Age have rolled on.

The Galadhrim clad themselves in brilliant golden armour that honours the great Mallorn trees that stand tall and proud within the forest. It is these standing armies that Lothlórien will need to call upon as Sauron’s power grows, whether to protect their own lands or to aid their allies in the fights against the servants of the Dark Lord. As the sun begins to set on the Third Age, the Elves of Lothlórien ready themselves for war, preparing to stand alongside the free peoples of Middle-earth one final time, now as they have always done.
