Harad

When the Dark Lord sent out the call for all those loyal to him to march upon the kingdom of Gondor, the armies of Harad were amongst the first to answer. Warriors from numerous tribes and great desert cities gathered into a single great army, putting aside their petty squabbles and tribal wars, and began the long march northwards to Minas Tirith and the Battle of Pelennor Fields.

The Haradrim are a callous and harsh people who have become hardy through their tough lives in the arid desert wastes of the southlands. Their warriors employ all manner of unusual tactics when they fight, coating their weapons in vile toxins that cause a slow and painful death for any who are so much as grazed by their blades or arrows. Such warfare may be looked down on by the other races of Men, but they cannot deny that the underhanded methods are effective.

Yet it is not their ruthless nature or use of toxins that make the Haradrim such a feared foe, it is the Mûmakil. These enormous, lumbering war beasts tower over the armies of the free peoples, dwarfing even the likes of buildings and fortress walls. Such beasts are easily capable of sweeping aside huge swathes of enemy ranks with a single swing of their mighty tusks, and their leathery hides protect them from all but the sharpest or most powerful weaponry. Those who find themselves in the path of these colossal monsters will almost certainly be trampled into the dirt.

When unleashed upon the Pelennor, the armies of Harad turn the tide of battle back in the favour of the Dark Lord once more, breaking the armies of Rohan and crushing what little spirit they still hold. In fact, had it not been for the timely arrival of Aragorn and the Army of the Dead, then the Haradrim would likely have seen the White City fall.
