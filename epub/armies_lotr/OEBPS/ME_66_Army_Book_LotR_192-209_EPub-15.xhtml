Wraiths on Wings

When the Nazgûl were sent forth from Minas Morgul to hunt the One Ring, they did so from the back of their dark steeds. Pursuing the Ringbearer from horseback, the Nazgûl chased Frodo all the way to the Bruinen, where the magic of Arwen invoked the great power of the river, calling it forth to wash away the Nazgûl. In doing so the Ringwraiths were left without their steeds – yet they had far more powerful beasts waiting within Mordor…

Setting out once more from the black lands, the Nazgûl do so this time from atop their monstrous Fell Beasts, for secrecy is no longer a luxury the Dark Lord can afford. These huge winged creatures are amongst the most terrifying in all of Mordor. Long necks protrude out from their bodies, and their maws are filled with rows of razor-sharp teeth. Their leathery wings span great distances, shrouding those below in shadow, and their talons are capable of piercing armour with frightening ease.

From atop their Fell Beasts, the Nazgûl can cover vast swathes of Middle-earth with great swiftness, quickly scouring the lands below them in search of the Ringbearer, travelling many leagues in a short space of time. The cry of a Fell Beast is a truly horrific sound, a guttural and primal screech that cuts through the resolve of all who hear it, freezing the hearts of even the bravest and most stalwart warrior.

Should a Fell Beast give chase to its quarry, no horse, Warg or other mount can hope to outrun them, escaping only if the creature should turn its attention elsewhere. Such is the might of these winged creatures that they can use their huge talons to destroy war machines or grasp many enemies in one go, either crushing them or lifting them high into the air before releasing them to plummet to their deaths below. Such is the terror of the Fell Beasts that it should come as little wonder that they make for the perfect mounts of untold horror for the deadliest of Sauron’s servants.
