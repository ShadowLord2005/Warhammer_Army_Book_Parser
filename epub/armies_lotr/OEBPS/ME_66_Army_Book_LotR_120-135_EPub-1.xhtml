Númenor

Although they were exiles following the drowning of their homeland at the end of the Second Age, the coming of the Men of Númenor to Middle-earth would change its fate forever. Founding the great kingdoms of Gondor and Arnor to replicate the glory and splendour of their former home, the Men of Númenor soon became a dominant force, fighting back the armies of the Dark Lord and becoming perhaps the greatest and most powerful kingdom within Middle-earth.

On the throne of Gondor and Arnor sat the High King, Elendil. Wise, noble and just, Elendil was a king beloved by his people and ruled in a manner befitting that of the great Númenórean kings of old. It was Elendil’s desire to see his new kingdom emulate the greatness of Númenor, a legacy that would be carried down by his son, Isildur, and by their descendants that shaped the kingdoms of Gondor and Arnor for centuries to come.

The sable-clad armies of Númenor quickly became a strong martial power. In their early years in Middle-earth, the forces of Númenor faced evil warlords that ruled the lands, driving the cruel Haradrim back to the southlands, and besting the fanatical Easterlings that sought to invade from the east – yet they prevailed in every instance, proving that their valorous deeds and Númenórean steel could win out over the cruelty and ruthlessness of lesser Men. However, these wouldn’t be the greatest threat to Elendil and his people…

When the hammer of Sauron fell upon Middle-earth, it was Gondor and Arnor that felt his wrath the hardest. Bands of Orcs raided the borders of Elendil’s lands, seeking to weaken the Men of Númenor and strike a sense of hopelessness into them. Such was the might of the forces of Mordor that even the armies of Númenor couldn’t face them alone, and so Elendil would have to form an alliance if his kingdom was to survive – one that would shape the future of Middle-earth.
