Uglúk’s Scouts

Having tracked Frodo and his companions to Amon Hen, Saruman’s scouting Uruk-hai have managed to engage and break the Fellowship. Their mission to capture the Halflings has resulted in Merry and Pippin being carried away and the valiant Boromir falling to the Uruks’ arrows in his efforts to protect the Hobbits from harm; and now the Uruk-hai make haste to deliver their prize to their master.

With the death of Lurtz, Uglúk has assumed command of the Uruk-hai as the strongest Uruk that now remains. Under Uglúk’s command are numerous scouting Uruk-hai, each one armed with swords or cleavers, and some carrying bows and quivers filled with black-fletched arrows. These Uruk-hai run as if the very whips of their master are behind them, racing towards Isengard with great haste and taking little respite for fear of failing the White Wizard.

The Orcs that have joined Uglúk’s party have been sent to ensure that the prize they carry is delivered. At the head of the Orcish ranks is Grishnákh, a wily Orc who detests the Uruk-hai and would gladly see them stuck with his own blade. This animosity is shared by all the Orcs that join him, including Snaga, a cunning Orc who spends most of his time looking out solely for himself and is always seeking an opportunity to further his own agenda.

This band of Orcs and Uruk-hai are only held together by the instructions of their masters, and they all harbour great disdain and distrust for each other. This manifests itself as a power struggle, with both Orcs and Uruk-hai alike trying to prove that they are strongest and most ruthless by slaying the most enemies; and they will fight all the harder to do so if the other is watching, no doubt sneering and making comments nearby.
