Kingdom of Rohan

Before the Wizard Saruman poisoned Théoden’s mind, the kingdom of Rohan had flourished under its king’s rule. Yet even then, Rohan found itself constantly needing to defend its borders against bands of Orcs and Wild Men of Dunland who sought to bring ruin upon them. The sons of Rohan are forced to become warriors and wield a blade far sooner than those of other races out of necessity.

Whilst Théoden rules from the city of Edoras, his armies are led by his royal bloodline and his most trusted marshals and captains. The king’s own son, Théodred, commands Rohan’s forces in battle, leading Éoreds of Riders of Rohan against their enemies. The prince is a fierce and capable warrior who has fought many battles in his short life, though his victories have instilled a great sense of pride in Théodred, one that can manifest as arrogance and may yet become the prince’s downfall.

Kingdom of Rohan

Beneath the Prince of Rohan are the Marshals of the Riddermark, the foremost of Rohan’s warriors, with each of them harbouring a great amount of battlefield experience and skill at arms. Perhaps the greatest of these is Éomer, the nephew of Théoden. An exceptional warrior and rider even by the standards of the Rohirrim, Éomer is unshakably loyal to his liege and will fight with all his might to protect the lands he has sworn his life to serve.

The pride of Rohan’s armies are undoubtedly her riders, which are the envy of the races of Men across Middle-earth. Few can boast the sheer levels of skill that the Riders of Rohan can bring, with each one being an expert combatant from horseback. Led by their captains, the Rohirrim will face any enemy that threatens their lands head-on, forging their own stories of daring and heroism that will rival even those of their forebears and the heroic deeds they achieved during the great history of Rohan.
