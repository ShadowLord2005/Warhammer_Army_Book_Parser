Return of the King

With the battle for the White City on the horizon, Gondor is short of allies. Though the Rohirrim ride south to war, their arrival may not be enough to break the armies of Mordor; they need more men. It is upon making camp in Dunharrow that Aragorn is met by Lord Elrond and presented with a unique gift: the shards of Narsil reforged into a new blade – Andúril, Flame of the West.

Yet this gift also comes with a task; to travel the Dimholt Road and confront those who dwell in the mountain. For those who still inhabit the mountain halls are the spirits of those long since dead, those who promised to aid Isildur against the armies of the Dark Lord millennia ago, yet broke their oath and were cursed to rot and linger onwards. Upon reaching the depths of Dunharrow, Aragorn confronts the King of the Dead and uses the reforged sword of the King of Gondor to hold the dead to their oath, gaining their allegiance for the battles to come.

When the Corsair ships pull into the docks of Harlond, it is not an army of Corsairs that leaps off the boats but instead a single Man, Elf and Dwarf. As the Orcs scoff at the three foes that stand before them, Aragorn summons the Army of the Dead to come forth, unleashing them upon the Orcs that stand before them catching them completely unawares. The sight of such a terrifying foe freezes the very hearts of the Orcish ranks, allowing the Dead to cut them down where they stand.

Upon the fields of the Pelennor, Aragorn unleashes the deadliest army in all of Middle-earth. The shadowed legions sweep across the Pelennor, slaying droves of Orcs, Haradrim and Mûmakil, and they will not cease their ghostly assault until they have made safe the city of Minas Tirith from the armies of the Dark Lord. Such an act will serve to finally honour what the King of the Dead swore to Isildur all those centuries ago, allowing Aragorn to hold their oath fulfilled and allow them to be at peace.
