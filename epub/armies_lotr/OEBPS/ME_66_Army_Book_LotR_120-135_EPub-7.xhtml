The Last Alliance

When the Dark Lord Sauron forged the Rings of Power, it wasn’t long before he began to dominate Middle-earth. One by one the free lands fell to the power of the Ring, each of them overrun by armies of Orcs sent by Sauron to destroy them. With each victory Sauron grew more powerful and it wasn’t long before the Dark Lord turned his gaze towards the greater powers in Middle-earth – yet there were some who resisted…

In order to combat the ever growing threat of Sauron, a last alliance of Men and Elves was formed between the high kings, Elendil and Gil-galad. Individually, both would have been but a mere roadblock for Sauron’s armies, but together they believed themselves to be strong enough to force back the relentless tides of Mordor and finally defeat the Dark Lord for good.

Marching upon Mordor, the Last Alliance of Men and Elves did battle side-by-side, and upon the slopes of Mount Doom they fought for the very fate of Middle-earth. Man and Elf arrayed against an Orc army that far surpassed their own in sheer size, showing no fear as the rabble raced towards them. Archers from both races rained arrows down upon their foes, whilst the front ranks stood stoically waiting for the Orcs to break upon their shield walls like waves on rock.

Though there have been many exceptional armies over the history of Middle-earth, few have gone down in legend as important as that of the Last Alliance. For had it not been for the sacrifice of Elendil and Gil-galad, and the armies of Men and Elves in defeating the Dark Lord, then the entirety of the free lands would have been plunged into shadow, changing the very fate of Middle-earth for the Ages to come.
