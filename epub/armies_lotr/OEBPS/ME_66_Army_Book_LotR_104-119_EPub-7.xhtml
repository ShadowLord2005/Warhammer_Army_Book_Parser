Reclamation of Osgiliath

The city of Osgiliath was once the great capital of Gondor, a shining beacon of the glory of the Men of Númenor rebuilt in Middle-earth. However, over the years of the Third Age, Osgiliath has been the target of many assaults from the forces of Mordor, causing it to fall into ruin and decay, becoming a crumbling shell of its former grandeur. Even towards the end of the Third Age, Osgiliath finds itself home to Orc bands using it for naught more than an outpost.

Refusing to let the former capital remain in Orcish hands, Denethor has commanded that the city of Osgiliath must be reclaimed for Gondor and entrusts this task to his eldest son, Boromir. Carrying the banner of Minas Tirith aloft, Boromir leads the warriors of the White City against the Orcs that have infested the city, inspiring his followers to great deeds of valour as they seek to free Osgiliath from Mordor’s grasp. Such is the fury that Boromir fights with that those that stand against him are swiftly cut down.

At Boromir’s side stands Faramir, his younger brother. Though he is not held in the same high esteem by their father, Faramir is still a valiant and exceptional warrior in his own right. It is Faramir who leads the Rangers of Gondor in the fights against Mordor, using their exceptional skills in tracking and moving swiftly to navigate the ruined streets and strike when they can catch the Orc armies unawares. Individually, both Boromir and Faramir are capable fighters, though together they are almost unstoppable.

Many of the Men who fight alongside the two brothers have joined them on many campaigns before, becoming knowledgeable in the battleplans and tactics employed by the two brothers, and allowing them to fight even more efficiently on the battlefield. Such is the loyalty that Boromir and Faramir inspire in those they lead into battle, that when they fight to liberate Osgiliath from Orcish hands they will do so with a grizzled determination; so long as the sons of the steward stand tall.
