Road to Rivendell

Having been gifted the One Ring by his uncle, Frodo has been tasked by Gandalf to deliver it to Rivendell and the safety of the Elves. To accompany him on this journey, Frodo brings with him some of his most trusted friends from the Shire: Samwise Gamgee, Meriadoc Brandybuck and Peregrin Took. Though they may not be initially aware of the dangers that will face them, the Hobbits will bravely stand by Frodo against any who threaten him.

Upon the Hobbits’ arrival in Bree, they make for the inn of the Prancing Pony, allowed entry to the town during the pouring rain and under the cover of darkness by the Gatekeeper of Bree. It is within the Prancing Pony where Frodo and his companions are able to enjoy their first good meal, large pints and warm hearth since they set out from the Shire, all of which are catered for them by the jolly innkeeper, Barliman Butterbur; who also manages to secure them a pony by the name of Bill.

Yet it is not just Barliman the Hobbits encounter in Bree. Within the shadows of the Prancing Pony a mysterious man is watching the Hobbits closely who, upon Frodo’s mishap and accidental slipping on of the Ring, reveals himself to be Strider, an ally of Gandalf. This Strider seems to know about the nature of the Ring and the terrifying Black Riders that hunt them, and offers to help Frodo and his companions to evade the clutches of the Nazgûl, guiding them through the wilderness and onwards to Rivendell.

Yet even Strider can not keep the Nazgûl off their trail entirely, and they eventually catch up to the Hobbits at Weathertop, resulting in Frodo being stabbed with a Morgul Blade. After driving the Nazgûl off, Strider attempts to slow the poison, only to be caught off-guard by the Elf-maid, Arwen. Knowing that the only chance for Frodo to survive is for him to reach Rivendell and the medicine her father can provide, Arwen rides with Frodo as fast as she can, though she will need to ride as swift as the wind if she is to outride the Nazgûl…
