Riders of Éomer

The siege of Helm’s Deep is well underway and things are looking bleak for the armies of Rohan. The Deeping Wall has been breached, a section reduced to rubble in an explosion of fire, Uruk-hai have flooded through the walls and the defenders of the fortress have been pushed back to the throne room. Yet even as hope is fading for those that remain within Helm’s Deep, help is not far away – for dawn is about to break on the fifth day…

Atop the hills that surround the Deeping-coomb, the valley that contains Helm’s Deep, as the morning light begins to crest the hills, Gandalf can be seen riding Shadowfax – and he has brought help with him. Having found Éomer and the rest of the exiled Rohirrim, Gandalf has led them with haste to defend the Hornburg from the amassed armies of the White Hand and, after riding through day and night, they have reached the battle just in time.

Riders of Éomer

The Marshal of the Riddermark rides with his Éored and other loyal Rohirrim who stood by Éomer when he was cast out under the orders of Gríma Wormtongue. Each of these riders is a warrior of great skill and prowess, trained in the art of warfare from horseback for many years, and have gained great experience fighting under the guidance of Éomer. Under the command of their Marshal, these riders have come to liberate their kin besieged within Helm’s Deep, and will give their all to ensure they do.

With a rousing shout of ‘To the King!’ from Éomer, the Rohirrim begin the rapid descent down the hill and towards the ranks of Uruk-hai below. The timing of their arrival was well prepared by Gandalf, for as they charge towards the fray the morning sunlight bursts over the peaks of the hills behind them, blinding the Uruk-hai and rendering them all but helpless against the ferocity of the Rohan charge.
