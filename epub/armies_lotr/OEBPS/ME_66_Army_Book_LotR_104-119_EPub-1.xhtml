Defenders of the Hornburg

Edoras has fallen, the king’s sons have been slain and the few Rohirrim who survive have been driven back to the fortress of the Hornburg where they are besieged by Wulf’s vast army of Dunlendings and Hill Tribesmen. The loss of his sons has driven Helm Hammerhand mad with grief, becoming a dishevelled wreck muttering under his breath, no longer fit to rule over his subjects as he once had; a state that sees the king seemingly disappear from the fortress itself.

With her father no longer capable of ruling, it is his daughter, Héra, who takes up command of the armies of the Hornburg. With her allies Olwyn and Lief at her side to provide her with counsel, Héra rallies those within the fortress to prepare for war. For though she may not be the king, the blood of Helm Hammerhand still runs through Héra’s veins, and she has been blessed to inherit his famous stoicism and natural ability to lead.

Defenders of the Hornburg

Those who defend the Hornburg against the rabble of Hill Tribesmen now camped outside their gates know that they are facing the greatest threat to Rohan’s existence in the kingdom’s history. Yet in the face of such impossible odds, the Rohirrim refuse to simply resign themselves to defeat. Instead, they steel themselves for the battles ahead and prepare to give their all for the defence of their kingdom, and to fight with every ounce of strength, courage and valour they can muster to avenge those cut down by Wulf’s armies.

Yet even in the blighted winter landscape, the defenders of the Hornburg do not fight entirely alone. Within the blizzards outside the fortress, an unknown force has been slaughtering Wulf’s men at night, picking off those who stray from the camp. Though the armies of Wulf believe the so-called beast to be a fearsome wraith, the truth is that even in madness Helm Hammerhand is a fearsome warrior and his grief has led him to avenge his fallen kin by any means necessary.
