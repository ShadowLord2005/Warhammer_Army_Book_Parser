Road to Helm’s Deep

His mind now freed from the clutches of Saruman, Théoden is made aware of all that has befallen his kingdom. Not only has he lost his only son, Théodred, but Rohan finds itself weakened and Edoras vulnerable to attack. Knowing that Saruman will not relinquish his hold on Rohan lightly, Gandalf advises Théoden to lead his people to the refuge of Helm’s Deep in order to weather the inevitable assault from the armies of Isengard.

Seeing there to be no other option if his people are to survive, Théoden orders Edoras to be evacuated and its people to begin the trek to Helm’s Deep. Any able men are tasked with protecting the convoy, riding ahead to scout out the path or holding back to ensure none of Saruman’s spies are following. Though Gandalf has gone to look for aid elsewhere, the Rohirrim are joined by Aragorn, Legolas and Gimli, all of whom pledge to protect the people they now ride with.

Yet the road to Helm’s Deep is a long and dangerous one. The convoy is slow and ponderous, containing many women, children and livestock as they trudge towards Helm’s Deep. It is this that makes the Rohirrim an easy target for attack – one which Saruman foresees. In a bid to slay the people of Rohan and to take control of the kingdom, Saruman commands his packs of Warg Riders to attack them on the road.

With their enemies bearing down upon them, Théoden leads his most capable warriors to meet them head-on – ensuring they are fighting away from the people he is aiming to protect. Armed with sword, spears and a steely determination, the riders under Théoden’s command crash into their enemies, using the full force of their cavalry charge to inflict great damage upon their foes – their sole aim to buy the people of Rohan enough time to reach the safety of Helm’s Deep.
