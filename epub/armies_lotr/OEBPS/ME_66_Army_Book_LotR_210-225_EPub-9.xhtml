Army of the White Hand

Before his treachery was fully revealed to the free peoples of Middle-earth, it was believed that the Wizard Saruman was still a force for good within the world, using his powers to aid those he once called allies. However, within his stronghold of Isengard, Saruman was secretly amassing an army, one that would be used to man the forges of industry beneath Orthanc, and also seek to weaken the kingdom of Rohan.

This army is made up of those who the Wizard managed to rally to his cause. Orcs have been sent to Isengard to aid Saruman in his schemes, working the forges and breeding pits. Packs of Warg Riders act as scouts, traversing the nearby lands to ensure none discover their master’s plans; and the Wild Men of Dunland have pledged themselves to Saruman to gain revenge on the Rohirrim they so despise.

Yet it is those grown beneath Orthanc that prove to be Saruman’s most potent warriors, his first batch of Uruk-hai. These strong, vicious warriors lead the raids on nearby Rohan villages and settlements, leaving naught but smouldering ruins in their wake. Such is the brutality of these Uruk-hai that Saruman has finally decided to reveal his true intentions, marking his warriors with the White Hand – a precursor to the full might Isengard would eventually unleash.

Having used his voice to weaken Théoden, Saruman’s armies now rampage almost unopposed across the lands of Rohan as they seek to bring the kingdom to its knees. Yet Saruman’s powers are not limited to mere whispers and conjurations, and can even be used to manipulate the elements from great distances, a power that makes the armies of Saruman a formidable foe.
