Minas Morgul

On the western border of Mordor lies the dead city of Minas Morgul, home of the Nazgûl and a great fortress of Mordor. Yet, Minas Morgul was not always the blighted city it is now, it was formerly a stronghold of Gondor. Known as Minas Ithil, the fortress acted as the first great bastion against the armies of Mordor, a stalwart line of defence put in place to keep the armies of the Dark Lord at bay.

That all ended when the Nazgûl descended upon the city, conquering it with their sorcerous might and transforming it into a place of dread. Now shrouded in darkness, Minas Morgul gives off an eerie green glow and has an evil feel to it. Those who stray too close to Minas Morgul are said to be overcome with a sense of great dread, for the Nazgûl have dwelt there for so long that their malign influence has seeped into not just the very city itself but also corrupted the surrounding lands.

Many stationed within Minas Morgul have also been imbued with the malign sorceries of the Nazgûl, their weapons seemingly more potent the longer they have lingered within the city. Alongside them march Orcs from the Morannon who stopped at the dead city before marching upon Osgiliath. These Orcs make for a formidable fighting force – the innate strength of those from the Morannon complementing the sinister weaponry of those who have long dwelt there.

Not all of Minas Morgul’s armies are of the living. Ghostly spectres are said to have been seen alongside the Orcs, tempting the living to stray from the path and into harm. Yet the deadliest warriors of Minas Morgul are undoubtedly the Nazgûl, and, above all, the Witch-king of Angmar. As Sauron’s most fearsome servant, the Witch-king leads the armies of the Dark Lord into battle from atop his dreaded Fell Beast, and when he rides out from Minas Morgul, the Witch-king is at the height of his power.
