Atop the Walls

With the forces of Mordor gathered upon the Pelennor, the city of Minas Tirith has been besieged. With Denethor’s mind having descended into madness with the loss of his son, command of the city and her forces has fallen to Gandalf. From atop the walls of Minas Tirith the White Wizard now has the task of rallying the warriors of Minas Tirith once more and steeling their resolve.

Yet all is not lost, Minas Tirith is still a nigh-on impenetrable fortress that has never yet been breached in its long history. Great war machines line the walls, providing the city with the means of fending off their attackers from afar. These imposing Battlecry Trebuchets are designed to launch huge chunks of rock and broken masonry towards their foes, blasting huge holes in the formations of Mordor and bringing down siege towers with a single hit. It is these war machines that Gandalf hopes can stave off the advancing Mordor forces for long enough for their allies to arrive.

However, should the walls of the city be breached, the seven layers of the city each have huge gates, and should one level be overrun then those who survive can fall back to the next and the defence can begin anew. Within the city walls, the Men of Minas Tirith will fight with renewed vigour, creating formations within the narrower streets to force the Orc armies away from vital areas and fighting all the harder to protect the people who make their home in the White City.

Though command of Minas Tirith’s armies has fallen to Gandalf, he is not the only hero to stand at the defence of the city. The Citadel Guard have joined the White Wizard in the defence, fulfilling their duty to protect it with their lives should the need arise. At their head is Irolas, the Captain of the Guard, who commands his troops with an unwavering sense of determination and courage, even in the face of such fearsome odds. Even Peregrin Took takes up arms, standing true to his oath sworn to the steward and fighting with all the strength he can muster in the defence of the White City.
