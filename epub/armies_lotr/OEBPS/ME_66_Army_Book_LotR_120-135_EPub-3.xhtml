Lindon

During the Second Age of Middle-earth, the greatest kingdom of the Elves is that of Lindon to the west of the Blue Mountains. It is here where the High Elves dwell, and are ruled over by Gil-galad, the High King of the Noldor. The Elves have ever been the enemies of darkness and have long fought to rid the world of the shadow of Morgoth, and in the later Ages, that of his servant, Sauron.

Following the forging of the Rings of Power and Sauron’s rise in the latter years of the Second Age, Gil-galad knew it was only a matter of time before war came to the Elves. Using the power of the Ring, Sauron crushed many of the kingdoms of the Elves, leaving a trail of ruined cities and broken bodies in his wake. When Elendil, the High King of Gondor and Arnor, came to Gil-galad with the proposition of forging an alliance to challenge the rule of Sauron, it was one that Gil-galad accepted readily and such an alliance may yet prove to be the downfall of the Dark Lord.

Within Lindon lies the Grey Havens, the Elven port which sees the ships sail to the Undying Lands, and it is here where the Elf lord Círdan dwells, overseeing the Elven ships that set sail from the port itself. Though he is well past his prime in terms of fighting, Círdan is still more than adept with the ways of magic, able to use his powers to aid his allies. When the Elves of Lindon are called to war, Círdan will gather his kin from the Grey Havens and march with them to aid the High King of the Noldor.

At the height of their power, the armies of Lindon are without peer within the free peoples of Middle-earth. The Elves that make up their ranks are accomplished warriors with blade, bow and spear, and each of them has seen countless battles over their immortal lives. Clad in gleaming golden armour, the sight of the armies of the High Elves is one that will make even the bravest Orc tremble, such is the renowned martial prowess of the Elves.
