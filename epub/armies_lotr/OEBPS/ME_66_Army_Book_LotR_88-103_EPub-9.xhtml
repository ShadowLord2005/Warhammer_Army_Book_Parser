Ride Out

With the Uruk-hai having stormed over the battlements of Helm’s Deep and slaughtered many of those who had strived to defend it, it seems as though victory is within the grasp of the armies of the White Hand. Those who still survive have fallen back to the keep, barring themselves within the throne room. A sense of hopelessness and despair has begun to seep into the minds of the survivors, and even King Théoden believes that their time has now come.

Yet Aragorn is not ready to give up the fight. Stirring Théoden with a moving speech of how the Rohirrim and their allies have fought and died to defend Helm’s Deep, Aragorn convinces the king to ride out one last time against the Uruk-hai that are attempting to break down the doors. Gathering their horses, Théoden and his allies prepare for one final charge in a bid to rout the fighting Uruk-hai – a charge that will result in either death or glory.

Ride Out

When the time is right, Gimli lets out a great blast on the Horn of the Hammerhand that echoes around the valley, signalling Théoden’s charge out from the doors of his keep. Aragorn and Legolas ride alongside Théoden, felling enemies from horseback with both blade and bow, giving their all to deliver Helm’s Deep from the onslaught of Isengard. The king’s Royal Guard are led by Gamling, who himself carries the Royal Standard of Rohan aloft. Should this be the last stand of the Rohirrim, then Gamling will ensure that the king’s colours fly for as long as possible.

Those who ride out alongside Théoden know that hope is all but lost, and this will likely prove to be their doom; yet they do so anyway in a final attempt to fight for freedom and break the Uruk-hai that have them besieged. For it is when the darkness is greatest that hope shines the most brightly, and so long as Helm’s Deep still has warriors willing to fight to defend it, then it shall not fall.
