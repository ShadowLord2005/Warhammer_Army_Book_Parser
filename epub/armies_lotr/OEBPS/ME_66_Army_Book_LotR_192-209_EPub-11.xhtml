Barad-dûr

It was during the years of the Second Age that the Dark Lord Sauron rose to full power. Though he had helped the free peoples create the Rings of Power, it was within the lands of Mordor where he forged for himself a master Ring to rule all others. Into this he poured his malice, his cruelty and his will to dominate all life, and through the power of the One Ring Sauron set out to conquer all of Middle-earth.

The armies of the Dark Lord are filled with creatures bound to his indomitable will. Legions of Orcs swarm the plains of Mordor, multiplying at horrific speeds and swelling Sauron’s forces. Armed with crude weaponry and plated in whatever scraps of metal the forges of Mordor can spare, these Orcs make up the bulk of the armies of Mordor and are sent in huge numbers to wage war upon the enemies of the Dark Lord, bringing death and ruin to the free peoples in their master’s name.

Barad-dûr

However, there are more than just mere Orcs within Sauron’s ranks. Vicious Wargs race into battle with their riders atop them, tearing at their foes with razor-sharp teeth and claws. Huge, monstrous Trolls stride into battle wielding massive cleavers or clubs, protected by thick scales and even thicker armour. And then there are the Nazgûl – Sauron’s most deadly of servants. Deceived and corrupted by Sauron, these wraith-like beings now serve the Dark Lord unwaveringly.

During the War of the Last Alliance, the Dark Lord himself strode into battle against the armies of Men and Elves. Such was the might of Sauron that none could stand against him, smashing aside his foes with great swings of his mace. Even the high kings Elendil and Gil-galad could not bring him down, and were felled by the Dark Lord’s fury. Only by separating the One Ring from its master could its power be undone…
