Besiegers of the Hornburg

Edoras now lies under the control of Wulf, who sits atop the throne of Rohan having declared himself king – though unable to ratify his claims. However, even with his victory and the deaths of Haleth and Háma by his hands, Wulf will not be content until the entire line of Helm Hammerhand is wiped from the very face of Middle-earth. To this end, Wulf commands his forces to march upon the fortress of the Hornburg and besiege it until those inside either perish or are forced to sally forth.

Wulf’s desire to see the death of Helm has become all-consuming, devouring any part of the person he was before his father’s death. Driven by rage, Wulf fights like a man possessed and will not relent once the fighting starts; commanding his forces to keep the fortress cut off despite their own diminishing supplies leading to losses. In order to keep his men content and willing to fight, Wulf promises them any riches found within the fortress – a prize that will sate the greed of any Hill Tribesman should they be victorious.

The weather around the Hornburg is blisteringly cold. Snow and ice covers the rocky ground making it hard to traverse easily, and blanketing the bodies of the fallen. Within the depths of night, a threat lingers in the darkness. A small patrol of Orcs searches the corpses around the Hornburg looking for something, bringing a fearsome creature in tow. Though they may not be directly in the service of Wulf, their goals certainly align temporarily, for both wish to see the fortress fall.

The besiegers of the Hornburg are perhaps the greatest Dunlending force ever mustered to this date. Teeming hordes of Hill Tribesmen rampage towards the fortress walls, each intent on bringing death and ruin to the people of Rohan. Should the armies of Wulf be successful, Wulf will finally have the revenge he so desperately craves for the death of his father, and he will be able to proclaim himself as the rightful king – an act that would have dire consequences for the rest of the Third Age of Middle-earth…
