Fangorn

At the close of the Third Age, Middle-earth is controlled largely by a handful of great kingdoms, each ruling over huge swathes of land. However, there are still many other ancient powers within Middle-earth, lurking within the forests and vales, free from the commands of the realms of Men, Elves and Dwarves. Such beings are content to simply avoid the wars and conflicts of the wider world, keeping themselves hidden and set apart.

Of these ancient lands, the forest of Fangorn is by far the oldest. Within the boughs of the great woodland dwell the Ents, the tree-shepherds of Middle-earth. Often resembling the very trees they live amongst, the Ents have been around since before the dawn of the First Age, wandering the woodland and forests of Middle-earth to protect them against any evil that would seek to corrupt them. However, in the dwindling years of the Third Age, the Ents are few in number and have taken to residing only within the forest of Fangorn itself.

The Ents give little to no thought to the world outside Fangorn, instead growing weary with the weight of millennia. As the decades have rolled onwards, the forest has become unruly, and the Ents are too few in number to control all the trees under their protection. With the Ents prone to sleeping for years at a time, they were oblivious to the rising threat of Isengard and also to the relentless march of Saruman’s flames of industry, resulting in the forest near Isengard being ripped up to fuel its forges.

It is upon the discovery of Saruman’s treachery that the Ents are finally stirred into action. Treebeard, the greatest of the tree-shepherds, lets forth a mighty call, rousing the slumbering Ents from their deep sleep and summoning them to march to war with Isengard. Though they may well be marching to their doom, the Ents are determined to seek revenge for their kind felled by Saruman’s armies and plan on breaking the dam and releasing the pent-up River Isen in order to wash away the filth of Saruman forever.
