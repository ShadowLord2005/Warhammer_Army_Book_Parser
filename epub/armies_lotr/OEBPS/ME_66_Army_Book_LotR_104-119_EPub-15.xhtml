Men of the West

Though the Battle of Pelennor Fields has been won, the free peoples of Middle-earth do not have the time to celebrate their victory. Though the forces of Mordor have been driven back, the Dark Lord has not yet been defeated and behind the Black Gate he begins to amass his forces once more for an even greater attack upon Gondor. In doing so, Sauron has unwittingly placed thousands of Orcs between Frodo and the fire of Mount Doom.

Within Minas Tirith, the leaders of the armies of the free peoples gather to discuss their options, and a plan is devised. They hope that by marching on the Black Gate they can draw out the armies of the Dark Lord, thereby emptying the lands of Mordor of Orcs. Such an act will likely result in the Men of the West all but forfeiting their own lives, but in doing so they will be able to provide Frodo with safe passage across the plains of Gorgoroth and one final chance to destroy the One Ring and end the power of the Dark Lord.

Gathering their remaining strength, Aragorn and Éomer marshal their forces and march upon the Black Gate. Warriors of Minas Tirith and Rohan now stand side-by-side with one another against seemingly insurmountable odds as hordes of Mordor Orcs pour from the Black Gate. With little choice but to give their all and fight, the Men of the West will sell their lives dearly to enact the plan of their lords; it is only by the courage of Men that the day can be won.

Yet the armies of Men do not stand alone, alongside them stands Aragorn’s allies and friends. Legolas and Gimli have endured beside Aragorn since the formation of the Fellowship in Rivendell, and Gandalf has returned to Middle-earth to help guide those who would see Sauron fall. Even Merry and Pippin have pledged their swords to the fight ahead. Together, Men, Elves, Dwarves, Wizards and even Hobbits will stand together in one final fight; the result of which will determine the very fate of Middle-earth – for good or evil…
