The Black Gate

With the Battle of Pelennor Fields over, the forces of Mordor have been driven back to the dark lands, and the captains of the West are preparing to march upon Mordor. Yet even as they do, the forces of the Dark Lord are regrouping. Behind the Ash Mountains, legions of Orcs are gathering; every stronghold and Orc camp has been emptied with all of them now ready to confront the army that dares to challenge the Dark Lord so openly.

As the Black Gate opens, the Mouth of Sauron rides out. An emissary of the Dark Lord himself, this Black Númenórean greets the captains of the west with proclamations of Frodo’s demise, before the full might of Mordor is unleashed upon them. From the bowels of the dark lands vast legions of Orcs lurch into battle, driven onwards by the implacable will of their master. The Orcs are accompanied by huge, heavily armoured Trolls that tower above their foes and carry great weapons capable of splitting their enemies in two.

At the head of the forces at the Black Gate is a hulking Mordor Troll Chieftain, larger and more bloodthirsty than any of its kind. This great Troll is easily capable of besting the mightiest heroes of the free peoples, swatting them aside in the name of the Dark Lord. With such a beast at the head of the armies of the Black Gate, any hero that stands against it will need a stout heart, an unflinching resolve and every ounce of skill they can muster if they are to stand any chance of survival.

Yet it is more than just the armies of Orcs on the ground that emerge from the Black Gate. As the armies of the west fight the impossible odds that are spewed forth from the Morannon, a deafening piercing shriek can be heard from above, heralding the arrival of the Nazgûl atop their Fell Beasts. The armies of the Black Gate represent every final ounce of power that the Dark Lord can muster, forming a truly formidable force that has the might to destroy all in their path.
