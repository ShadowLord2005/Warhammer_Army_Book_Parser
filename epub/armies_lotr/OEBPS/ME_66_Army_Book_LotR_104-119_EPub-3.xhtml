Minas Tirith

Before the War of the Ring and the return of Aragorn, the White City of Minas Tirith was ruled over by the stewards, noble Men who were tasked with leading the great realm following the fall of the line of kings, until such a time that her rightful heir returned. In the final years of the Third Age, this duty has fallen to Denethor, a Man of noble blood though one whose desire for power runs deep; so deep that he may well be unwilling to relinquish the rule of the city should its king return.

Under the command of Lord Denethor, Minas Tirith’s armies have long held back the forces of Mordor, becoming the first line of defence between the shadowed lands and the free peoples of Middle-earth. Yet constant war has taken its toll on Minas Tirith; the armies of the White City have run thin with their blood being spilt in wars with Mordor. Such conflict has also had an impact upon the steward himself, with Denethor turning to one of the lost seeing stones for guidance; an act that begins to crack his mind.

Though at constant war, the armies of Minas Tirith are still a mighty force to be reckoned with. Each of their warriors is an accomplished fighter, trained from a young age to wield a blade and fight in formation. Clad in gleaming silver plate and carrying thick tower shields, the warriors of the White City form a nigh-on impenetrable bastion against the armies of the Dark Lord, standing strong against the blades of the enemy whenever they are called upon to fight.

Yet it is not just these warriors that make up the armies of Minas Tirith. The most elite fighters are called up to join the ranks of the Citadel Guard or even the Guard of the Fountain Court. The White City also boasts an impressive cavalry division, with each of their knights armed with finely-balanced and razor-sharp lances. Yet it is those that lead the city’s armies that set Gondor apart from other realms of Men; heroes such as Boromir and Faramir are amongst the greatest the race of Men have to offer in the fight against the Dark Lord.
