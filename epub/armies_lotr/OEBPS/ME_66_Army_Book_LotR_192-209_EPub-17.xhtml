Army of Gothmog

At the close of the Third Age, Sauron prepares to wage open war upon the free peoples of Middle-earth. His hammer will fall first and hardest upon the city of Minas Tirith and the kingdom of Gondor, who have long opposed the Dark Lord. To bring ruin to the race of Men, Sauron unleashes the greatest army of Orcs he can muster, sent with the sole purpose of razing the White City to the ground.

At the head of this army is his lieutenant, Gothmog, the highest ranking Orc in the service of the Dark Lord. Though he is scarred and no longer the formidable warrior he once was, this cruel and twisted Orc is without peer within the armies of Mordor. Few Orcs possess the keen grasp of battle tactics that Gothmog boasts, for he is the master of any battlefield he steps onto. Commanding the forces of Mordor with a ruthless tenacity, Gothmog’s sole purpose is now to end the race of Men.

Within Gothmog’s grand army are amassed legions of Morannon Orcs; stronger and tougher than the standard Mordor Orcs, these vicious killers are clad in thicker armour and carry keener weapons than their counterparts. Striding alongside the Orcs are the hulking Mordor Trolls; some wield great weapons to smash down walls and crush their enemies, whilst others push great war machines into battle that will be used to rain down huge rocks upon the battlements of Minas Tirith.

It is these forces that stormed the city of Osgiliath, overrunning it and bringing death to those who sought to defend it, claiming it for the Dark Lord once more. Following the sacking of Osgiliath, Gothmog has unleashed his armies upon Minas Tirith in the greatest battle of the Third Age. Such is the might of the army of Gothmog that it would take the combined armies of the free peoples to halt its relentless march and overcome it.
