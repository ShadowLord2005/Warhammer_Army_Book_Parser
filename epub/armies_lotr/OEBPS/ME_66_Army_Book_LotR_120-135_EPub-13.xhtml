The Eagles

High up in the peaks of the Misty Mountains nest the Great Eagles. These ancient and noble beings see themselves as above the petty squabbles and goings-on of those beneath their lofty perch, seeing them as little more than ants they need not concern themselves with. Yet the Great Eagles are far more than mere beasts, possessing an incredible intellect and fierce predatory instinct – a combination seldom seen within Middle-earth.

As it turns out, there are few in Middle-earth that are aware of the Great Eagles’ existence, and fewer still who can say they have ridden upon these proud beasts. The Eagles are not quick to trust those they meet, and those that come face-to-face with them must be cautious to prove they are an ally or risk being torn to shreds by their huge, razor-sharp talons. It is said that only the Wizards have earned true friendship with the Eagles, though quite how this friendship was formed is a mystery to all but the Istari and the Great Eagles themselves.

The Eagles

This friendship has proved fruitful a number of times during the latter years of the Third Age. When the company of Thorin Oakenshield found themselves trapped by Azog’s Hunters, it was the Eagles who rescued them from certain death. The Eagles also came to aid the free peoples of Middle-earth at the Battle of the Five Armies and the Black Gate, doing their part to rid Middle-earth of darkness. When Gandalf found himself trapped atop Orthanc, it was Gwaihir, the Lord of the Eagles, who rescued his friend from the clutches of Saruman, flying him away to safety.

Though the Great Eagles may wish to remain apart from the goings-on beneath their roost, the growing threat of Sauron means that even the Eagles will be forced to do their part for good. For should the Dark Lord prevail and reclaim the One Ring, then Middle-earth will be wreathed in shadow and the Eagles will suffer the same fate as all the other races of Middle-earth.
