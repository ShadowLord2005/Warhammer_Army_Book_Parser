The Fellowship

In all the long history of Middle-earth, there are few heroes as celebrated and admired as the legendary Fellowship of the Ring. Formed at the Council of Elrond in Rivendell, this unlikely band of nine companions pledged to do the impossible – to journey to the depths of Mordor and cast the One Ring into the fires of Mount Doom, destroying it and freeing Middle-earth from the darkness of Sauron.

Led by the wandering Istari, Gandalf the Grey, the Fellowship contained representatives from all the races of the free peoples. From the race of Men, a ranger of the north going by the name of Strider, and the noble Boromir from the city of Minas Tirith. From the Dwarves, the stubborn and steadfast Gimli, son of Glóin, a formidable warrior in his own right. And from the Elves, Legolas Greenleaf of the woodland realm, who would ensure that the Elves would finally see the power of the Ring ended, as it should have been over 3,000 years before.

The Fellowship

However, most importantly, the Fellowship contained four Hobbits – the most unlikely creatures to join such an epic quest. Frodo Baggins, joined by Samwise Gamgee, Meriadoc Brandybuck and Peregrin Took, set out to deliver the One Ring to Rivendell, only to be thrust into an adventure beyond their wildest imaginations. For the Hobbits who had never left the borders of the Shire, the journey would be filled with perils they’d never encountered before, and would take every ounce of courage they could muster to succeed.

The journey to Mordor would prove to be one fraught with danger, and everywhere the servants of the enemy would seek to track down and slay the Fellowship in a bid to reclaim the One Ring for their dark master. Yet hope would endure so long as the Fellowship stayed true to each other; though they would each be pushed to their limits on every step of their journey, and not all of them would live to see the end of their tale…
