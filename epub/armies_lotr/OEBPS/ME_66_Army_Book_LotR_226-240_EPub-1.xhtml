Muster of Isengard

With the Rohirrim having fled to Helm’s Deep, Saruman gathers together the full force of his armies at Orthanc. As an army of over ten thousand fighting Uruk-hai stands before him, Saruman instils in them a relentless battle frenzy and commands them to march upon Helm’s Deep. It is there that they plan to wage war against Théoden and all those who now garrison Helm’s Deep in the hope of defending it.

The army that Saruman unleashes is made up primarily of his Uruk-hai, strong and heavily armoured warriors that care little for anything other than the wars for which they were bred. Possessing great endurance, their relentless march towards Helm’s Deep is swift and unimpeded. Amongst the ranks of the Uruk-hai are groups of Berserkers; warriors who cover themselves in blood and shun the protection of armour. These warriors are some of the most deadly in the White Wizard’s army.

When the ranks of Uruk-hai engage in battle, they will fight with untold ferocity, caring little for the lives of their allies and continuing to fight even if those nearby are slain. Enemy swords bounce off the thick armour and broad shields of these fighting Uruk-hai, allowing them to press their advantage and cut down the enemies of the White Wizard that stand opposing them. Though Saruman’s fighting Uruk-hai have only existed for a short time, they possess remarkable martial skill.

With the White Wizard guiding them, the full muster of Isengard is perhaps one of the greatest armies in the closing years of the Third Age. Few can hope to match Saruman’s armies in terms of numbers, and fewer still can hope to match the sheer strength, endurance and lust for war that the Uruk-hai possess. It would take an army led by the greatest heroes of the Third Age to have a hope of defeating such a force.
