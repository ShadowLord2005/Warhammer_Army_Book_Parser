Riders of Théoden

As the Battle of Pelennor Fields rages on, things are looking increasingly bleak for the city of Minas Tirith – its gates have been breached, its walls scaled and now hordes of Orcs have begun to flood the lower levels. Yet as dawn breaks over the Pelennor, from the north the sound of horns can be heard ringing out across the crisp morning air, heralding the arrival of the Riders of Théoden.

As the sun rises over the Pelennor, and the Orcs seek to press the advantage, thousands of Riders of Rohan come into view from over the hills to the north, their spears gleaming in the morning sun. At the head of the army is King Théoden, clad in his royal armour. With his men behind him and the hordes of Mordor before him, Théoden gives a rousing speech for the ages, one that will stir the hearts of all who ride with him to untold acts of courage and turn even the most craven hearted into steadfast and resolute warriors.

With a series of war cries, the king leads the charge headlong towards the fray. At his side are his most trusted marshals and captains: his nephew Éomer, a mighty warrior in his prime, Gamling, the head of his Royal Guard who carries the colours of the king aloft, and, unbeknownst to Théoden, Éowyn rides into battle disguised as one of his riders, carrying the Hobbit Merry with her as she does so. Each of these will fight to the death for their king and will all achieve acts of great heroism in the battle to come.

When the Riders of Théoden charge into battle, there are few who can stand against their fearsome charge – its full force is enough to smash aside shield walls, trample their enemies underfoot and scatter those who have the sense to flee. The sight of such an awesome charge is enough to rally any ally to their cause with renewed hope and rout those who stand against them.
