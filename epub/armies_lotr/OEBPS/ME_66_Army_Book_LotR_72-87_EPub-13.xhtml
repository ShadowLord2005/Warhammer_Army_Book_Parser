The Shire

The Hobbits of the Shire are a peaceful race, quite content to ignore, and be ignored by, the comings and goings of the world outside their borders. In fact, many in Middle-earth are oblivious to the existence of Hobbits entirely, and those that are aware of Hobbits see them as of little or no relevance at all. Not that this troubles the Hobbits in the slightest, and they are quite happy to just go about their simple life without being bothered by the ‘big-folk’.

On the whole, the Hobbits of the Shire live a quaint, idyllic lifestyle away from the hustle and bustle of towns and cities, enjoying the simple things life offers such as sampling good food, the brewing of ales and the smoking of pipe-weed. However, where their hearts truly lie is in peace and quiet and good tilled earth, for all Hobbits share a love of things that grow – and it is for these reasons that a Hobbit makes for an excellent gardener.

Yet if there is one thing Hobbits enjoy even more than peace and quiet, it’s a good party filled with merriment, laughter and, of course, fireworks. Perhaps the most notable of such events was the 111th birthday of one Mr Bilbo Baggins; half the Shire was said to be invited, and the other half seemingly turned up anyway. Even Bilbo’s disgruntled relatives, the Sackville-Baggins, made an appearance, Lobelia proving to be particularly unpleasant as she ranted the ear off of Bilbo.

The fireworks were provided by the wandering Wizard, Gandalf the Grey. Gandalf has long been an ally of the Shire, having visited its rolling green hills many times over the years, and became known for aiding them in times of need. However, in more recent times Gandalf has been labelled as a disturber of the peace, possibly in relation to his part in Bilbo’s adventures with the Dragon, and him coming back far more ‘odd’ than when he left. Regardless, Gandalf will still come to the Shire should the Hobbits ever be in true need.
